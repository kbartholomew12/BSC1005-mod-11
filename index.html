Your Mission

You are part of a virtual tumor board. Your task is to review a simulated patient file and record a 2-minute diagnostic pitch explaining why this patient’s cell cycle regulation failed.

Important: This is a biology learning simulation, not a medical diagnosis activity. Focus on Chapter 8 concepts: checkpoints, proto-oncogenes, tumor suppressor genes, apoptosis, telomerase, chromosomal rearrangements, and cancer-cell characteristics.
What you will submit in the discussion
  1. A 2-minute video or audio diagnostic pitch using Kaltura/MyMedia in Canvas.
  2. Two peer consultation notes recommending a treatment mechanism for two classmates’ patient files.
What your diagnostic pitch should explain
  • The main cell cycle control problem.
  • The gene or process involved.
  • How the mutation or abnormal result allows cells to keep dividing.
  • Which tumor characteristics are shown in the file.
  • How the case connects to cancer development.

Kaltura / MyMedia

How to Record and Post Your Video in Canvas

Your course may call this tool Kaltura, My Media, or MyMedia. Use the MyMedia menu link in Canvas if your instructor has enabled it.
Recording steps
  1. Open your Canvas course.
  2. Select the MyMedia or My Media menu link.
  3. Select Add New.
  4. Choose the recording option your instructor recommends, such as Kaltura Capture, webcam recording, or media upload.
  5. Record your 2-minute tumor board handoff.
  6. Save and upload the recording to MyMedia.
  7. Return to the discussion post.
  8. Use the Canvas rich content editor to embed or attach your MyMedia video according to your instructor’s directions.
Before you submit
  • Check that your video/audio plays correctly.
  • Make sure your patient file is identified.
  • Use specific evidence from your simulation.
  • Keep the recording close to 2 minutes.
  • Do not read a generic definition list. Explain the case.

Consultation Note

Treatment Menu

Mechanism Options

Option 1: Taxane-Based Chemotherapy

Mechanism: Stabilizes microtubules and disrupts normal spindle function during mitosis.

Best fit: Rapid mitosis, many dividing cells, or mitosis/spindle-related problems.

Option 2: BH3 Mimetics

Mechanism: Inhibits anti-apoptotic BCL-2 proteins, allowing damaged cells to trigger apoptosis.

Best fit: High BCL-2, low apoptosis, or damaged cells surviving when they should die.

Option 3: Telomerase Inhibitor Agents

Mechanism: Blocks telomerase from maintaining chromosome ends.

Best fit: Very high telomerase activity or cells avoiding the normal division limit.

Option 4: Radiation Therapy

Mechanism: Damages DNA in cancer cells.

Best fit: DNA damage vulnerability or localized tumor growth.

Option 5: Targeted Growth-Signal Therapy

Mechanism: Blocks overactive growth signaling from an abnormal proto-oncogene.

Best fit: Overactive HER2 or similar growth-signal pathway.

Option 6: Immunotherapy

Mechanism: Helps the immune system recognize or attack cancer cells.

Best fit: Tumors with many mutations or abnormal cells avoiding immune detection.

Option 7: Cell Cycle Inhibitor

Mechanism: Slows movement through key parts of the cell cycle, especially G1 into S phase.

Best fit: Failed cell cycle brakes or cells entering DNA replication too easily.
